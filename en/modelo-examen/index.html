DELE C2 Exam Model

The DELE C2 is the highest level of the Diploma of Spanish as a Foreign Language. Unlike other levels, C2 integrates language skills into combined tests. Here is the complete structure.

Test 1: Language Use, Reading and Listening Comprehension

Duration: 105 minutes · Tasks: 6

Task 1 — Reading Comprehension

Answer multiple-choice questions about a complex informational text of about 450 words.

Task 2 — Reading Comprehension

Complete a text with extracted fragments, demonstrating understanding of textual cohesion.

Task 3 — Reading Comprehension

Identify in which texts certain ideas or information can be found.

Task 4 — Language Use

Complete a text with the correct lexical or grammatical options.

Task 5 — Listening Comprehension

Answer questions about extensive conversations or monologues.

Task 6 — Listening Comprehension

Match interventions with statements or identify specific information in complex audios.

Test 2: Integrated Skills — Listening and Reading Comprehension + Written Expression

Duration: 150 minutes · Tasks: 3

Task 1 — Writing from Listening

Listen to a lecture or presentation and write a formal text (report, article) integrating the information heard.

Task 2 — Writing from Reading

Read several texts and write an argumentative text integrating the ideas from the sources.

Task 3 — Review and Correction

Identify and correct errors in a text, demonstrating mastery of the linguistic norm.

Test 3: Integrated Skills — Reading Comprehension + Oral Expression

Duration: 20 minutes (+ 30 min preparation) · Tasks: 3

Task 1 — Sustained Monologue

Present a topic based on supporting texts, organizing a coherent and well-structured speech.

Task 2 — Debate

Discuss with the interviewer about the presented topic, defending and qualifying opinions.

Task 3 — Negotiation

Participate in a communicative situation that requires negotiating, persuading, or solving a problem.

Scoring

Grading System

Maximum score: 100 points. A minimum of 60% is required in each test (20 points per test) to pass. The result is PASS or FAIL.
