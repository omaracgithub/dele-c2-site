DELE C2 Oral Expression: Explained Tasks

Test 3 of the DELE C2 evaluates your ability to express and interact orally at the highest level. You have 30 minutes of preparation and 20 minutes of examination in front of two examiners. It is the test that generates the most nerves, but with the right preparation, you can approach it with confidence.

Structure of Test 3

Preparation Phase (30 minutes)

Before entering the exam room, you receive reading material (texts, graphics, images) related to a topic. During these 30 minutes, you can:

  • Read the provided texts.
  • Take notes on a paper that you can bring to the exam.
  • Organize your oral presentation.
  • Prepare arguments for the subsequent debate.

You cannot use a dictionary, phone, or any external material.

Examination Phase (20 minutes)

The oral exam is divided into three parts:

Task 1: Monological Presentation (6-7 minutes)

You must make a structured oral presentation on the proposed topic, based on the texts read during preparation. It is expected that you:

  • Present the topic clearly and contextually.
  • Expose the main ideas from the source texts.
  • Provide your own assessment and argumentation.
  • Conclude coherently.

Recommended Structure for the Monologue

Introduction (1 minute):

  • Present the topic: "The topic I am going to address is..."
  • Briefly contextualize: why it is relevant.
  • Anticipate the structure: "First, I will analyze..., then..."

Development (4-5 minutes):

  • Present 2-3 main ideas with arguments and examples.
  • Reference the texts: "According to the text from...", "As noted in..."
  • Contrast perspectives if the texts present different positions.
  • Add your own experience or knowledge when relevant.

Conclusion (1 minute):

  • Summarize your position.
  • Offer a final reflection or proposal.
  • Close naturally: "Ultimately...", "In conclusion..."

Tips for the Monologue

  • Do not read your notes: Use them as a guide, but maintain eye contact with the examiners.
  • Control the time: Practice so that your monologue lasts between 6 and 7 minutes. Not more, not less.
  • Vary your intonation: A monotonous monologue is boring. Use pauses, emphasis, and changes in rhythm.
  • Do not memorize: Examiners can detect memorized speeches. Be natural and flexible.

Task 2: Debate with the Examiner (5-6 minutes)

After your monologue, one of the examiners will initiate a debate with you on the same topic. They may:

  • Ask you to elaborate on a point from your presentation.
  • Present an opposing position for you to argue against.
  • Ask you questions about aspects you did not mention.
  • Pose hypothetical situations related to the topic.

Strategies for the Debate

  • Listen actively: Do not prepare your response while the examiner is speaking. Listen until the end and then respond.
  • Do not be afraid to disagree: The debate evaluates your argumentative ability, not whether you agree with the examiner.
  • Use nuances: "I understand your point, however...", "While it is true that..., we cannot ignore that..."
  • Ask for clarifications if necessary: "Are you referring to...?" is perfectly acceptable and demonstrates natural interaction.
  • Stay calm: If you do not know something, acknowledge it gracefully: "I do not have concrete data on that, but based on my experience..."

Task 3: Negotiation or Resolution (5-6 minutes)

The examiner presents you with a situation that requires negotiation, decision-making, or problem-solving. You must interact naturally, proposing solutions and responding to the examiner's proposals.

Strategies for Negotiation

  • Propose alternatives: Do not limit yourself to accepting or rejecting. Offer options: "We could consider...", "Another possibility would be..."
  • Justify your proposals: Each suggestion should have an argument behind it.
  • Show flexibility: "I would be willing to... as long as...", "I could accept that if..."
  • Seek agreement: The goal is to reach a solution, not to win an argument.

Common Topics in Test 3

The topics of the DELE C2 are complex and current:

  • Globalization and its social and economic effects.
  • Technology, artificial intelligence, and ethics.
  • Environment, sustainability, and climate change.
  • Education: models, reforms, access.
  • Media and misinformation.
  • Cultural diversity, migration, and integration.
  • Public health, well-being, and healthcare systems.
  • Art, culture, and heritage.
  • Work, employment, and labor transformation.
  • Science, research, and dissemination.

How to Make the Most of the 30 Minutes of Preparation

Time management during preparation is crucial:

  • Minutes 1-5: First quick reading of all texts. Identify the central theme and positions.
  • Minutes 5-15: Second selective reading. Highlight key ideas, data, and arguments you want to use.
  • Minutes 15-25: Organize your monologue. Write an outline with 3-4 main points and the conclusion.
  • Minutes 25-30: Prepare possible questions for the debate. Think of counterarguments to your own position.

Evaluation Criteria

Examiners evaluate:

  • Coherence and fluency: Organized speech, without excessive pauses or fillers.
  • Range and control: Varied and precise vocabulary, correct and complex grammar.
  • Interaction: Ability to respond, argue, and negotiate naturally.
  • Pronunciation and prosody: Natural intonation, appropriate rhythm, clear pronunciation.
